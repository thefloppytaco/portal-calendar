  3. OAuth consent screen → External → fill the required fields → Publish app (production — the "unverified app" warning at sign-in is expected for a personal project; in Testing mode the connection would expire weekly).

Do it all on a computer — this page works there too, so the long link never has to touch your phone:

  1. On the computer, open this same page: go to in any browser.
  2. In another tab, open calendar.google.com → Settings, signed in as the family member.
  3. Under “Settings for my calendars”, click their calendar → Integrate calendar.
  4. Copy “Secret address in iCal format” (ends in basic.ics) and paste it in the form here.

Phone-only alternative: open the settings link above in Safari (long-press → Open in Safari if the Google app grabs it), tap aA → Request Desktop Website, then follow steps 3–4.

Apple / iCloud Calendar — get the link
  1. On that person's iPhone, open the Calendar app.
  2. Tap Calendars at the bottom, then ⓘ next to their calendar.
  3. Turn on Public Calendar, tap Share Link… → Copy.
  4. Come back here and paste it above (webcal://… links are fine).
  1. Only calendars the person owns in iCloud can be shared this way — the built-in Birthdays/Holidays calendars can't, and a Google account synced into the iPhone should use the Google steps instead.
Magic words — send things from any calendar

Create a calendar event anywhere (your phone, work laptop…) with one of these titles, and the Portal hides it from the calendar and routes it instead:

  1. todo: pick up dry cleaning → the "To-Do" list
  2. groceries: oat milk → the "Groceries" list
  3. chore: Chani water the plants → a one-time chore on the event's date — a family member's name in the text assigns it to them

Typos are forgiven ("groceres:" still finds Groceries), lists are created automatically if they don't exist, and each event is processed once — it stays in your real calendar as a record. The Portal sees it on its next feed sync (within ~15 min).

Even better: add a dedicated Inbox calendar (see "Add a calendar"). In an Inbox, no prefixes are needed — plain phrases like add oat milk to groceries, buy batteries, or remind Chani to water the plants just work, and nothing from it ever shows on the board.

Good to know
  1. These links give read-only access to the calendar — treat them like passwords and only paste them here.
  2. There's no way to fetch the link automatically: neither Google nor Apple exposes it to other apps — each person copies theirs once, then it syncs forever.
  3. New events appear on the Portal within ~15 minutes (Google/Apple can also take a few minutes to publish changes). Use “Sync now” to pull immediately.
